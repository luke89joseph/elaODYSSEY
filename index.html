The slaughter of the suitors is a climatic moment in The Odyssey, where Odysseus, disguised as a beggar, finally reveals his identity and brings vengeance upon the suitors. With the help of his son Telemachus, loyal servants, and Athena, Odysseus locks the suitors in the great hall. Armed with his bow, he begins by killing their leader, Antinous, shooting him in the throat, and then systematically slaughters the rest in a brutal and decisive battle. The suitors are unarmed and overwhelmed, as Telemachus and the servants provide weapons to Odysseus and guard against escape. After the battle, Odysseus ensures loyalty in his household by sparing the faithful and punishing the traitorous servants.
